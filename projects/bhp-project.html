Big History Project

Context

The Big History Collaboration Project was a year-long experience where we worked together to explore key turning points in human history, called thresholds. Our class looked at different themes, and our group chose to focus on immigration as a lens to understand how people, societies, and systems have changed over time.

  • Studied major historical thresholds,from the Big Bang to the Modern Revolution
  • Connected those thresholds to the theme of immigration
  • Conducted deep historical research
  • Reflected on personal and societal impacts of migration
  • Analyzed both past patterns and possible futures of immigration

Content

Our group focused on immigration stories and the systems surrounding them. We explored:

  • Historical immigration waves across different thresholds
  • Why people migrate (push and pull factors)
  • How laws and policies changed over time
  • Personal stories of immigrants and the impact on identity

To bring it all together, we used Google Slides, Canva, and timeline tools to create an interactive presentation. We split up tasks, set weekly goals, and checked in regularly to make sure everyone stayed on track. This helped us learn how to manage a long-term project and work together as a team.

Challenges

Some of our biggest challenges included:

  • Finding primary sources that felt meaningful and weren’t repetitive
  • Organizing timelines clearly to match historical thresholds
  • Working through team communication issues during busy weeks

Takeaways

This project taught me a lot about collaboration, research, and empathy. Listening to immigrant stories made the topic personal, and connecting it to Big History thresholds gave me a deeper understanding of how migration has always shaped human development. I also improved at organizing complex information and presenting it in a way that others could follow.

Next Steps

In future projects, I want to continue improving how I explain complex ideas clearly — whether it’s in slides, websites, or visuals
